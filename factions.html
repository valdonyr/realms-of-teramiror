Factions of Teramiror

Across Teramiror, alliances form and tensions simmer beneath the surface. While peace still holds, it does so uneasily—whispers of change and uncertainty can be heard in courts, markets, and borderlands alike.

Alberon & Rhuedon

Alberon and Rhuedon stand aligned in their interests, maintaining a close political and military relationship. Their cooperation has grown steadily over the years, shaping the balance of power across the region.

In recent times, their movements have drawn increased attention. Activity along their borders has become more deliberate, and their internal affairs more guarded.

Though no open conflict has yet begun, many believe that Alberon and Rhuedon are preparing for something significant—what form that may take, however, remains uncertain.

The Great Alliance: Faningor & Kalak

Faningor and Kalak are bound by a long-standing alliance rooted in cooperation and shared interests. Together, they have maintained stability across their territories for many years.

Recently, however, both kingdoms have grown increasingly watchful. Subtle changes in the actions of neighboring powers have not gone unnoticed.

While no immediate threat has been declared, preparations have quietly begun. Scouts have been sent, defenses reviewed, and communication between the two allies strengthened.

There is a growing sense that something may be unfolding beyond what is immediately visible—though its nature remains unclear.

Varnonel

Varnonel maintains a stance of neutrality in the broader tensions between the great powers. Its position allows it to act as a hub of trade and diplomacy, navigating carefully between competing interests.

Its economic strength is bolstered by strong trade relationships, particularly with the Dwarves of the Iron Hills. Together, they sustain a steady flow of goods and resources that benefit both societies.

Despite its outward stability, rumours persist of hidden dealings within certain parts of Varnonel—whispers of shadowed markets and discreet exchanges with unknown parties. None of these claims have been confirmed.

The Dwarves of the Iron Hills

Beneath the Iron Hills lies a vast and enduring dwarven civilization. Renowned for their craftsmanship and resilience, the dwarves have built a society that thrives both independently and through trade.

Their connection with Varnonel has brought prosperity to both, allowing for the steady exchange of materials, knowledge, and goods.

Though largely focused on their own domains, the dwarves remain aware of the shifting balance of power above—and below.

The Drow of the Underdark

Beneath the mountains, deep within the Underdark, dwell the drow—an ever- present and often unseen force in the world.

Traditionally confined to the depths, their presence has in recent times become more noticeable. Sightings on the surface, though still rare, have grown more frequent.

The reasons for this shift remain unknown. Some believe the drow are responding to changes within the world itself—sensing disturbances long before others become aware of them.

A World on the Edge

Across Teramiror, subtle changes have begun to emerge. Seasons behave strangely, patterns shift, and the natural order seems—at times—unsteady.

Yet for most, these signs are overshadowed by the more immediate concerns of politics and potential conflict.

Still, a quiet question lingers: are these events connected—or merely coincidence?
